# Product Process
- **Product team** defines and maintains product quality expectations (outcomes, acceptance criteria, and prioritisation of fixes).
- Acceptance criteria issues (missing, unclear, incorrect, or not testable) are product/requirements issues and must be resolved by the product team (**PO/BA**, with **PM** guidance).
- For ambiguous issues (requirement interpretation vs implementation), product and development jointly triage and agree the classification before scheduling work.
## Process

- **Product Manager** works with **Stakeholders** to understand business needs.
- **Stakeholders** sit above the flow, providing business needs to the **Product Manager** and receiving feedback.
- **Product Manager**, **Technical Authority**, **Product Owner**, and **UI/UX (prototype)** collaborate in a continuous, bidirectional loop to shape ideas, test feasibility, and refine user experience before committing to high level direction.
- Within that loop, **Technical Authority** keeps feasibility visible, **Product Owner** refines the high level requirements, and **UI/UX (prototype)** helps explore and validate solution ideas; everyone exchanges feedback both ways.
- Once the direction is clear enough, the **Product Owner** passes the high level requirements to the **Business Analyst**.
- **Business Analyst** and **UI/UX** work together to refine high-level requirements into clear, testable lower-level requirements with acceptance criteria. They regularly consult with the **Support Team** during this phase to ensure prototypes, designs, and acceptance criteria reflect real-world user scenarios. They also loop with the **Product Owner** for clarification and the **Engineering Team** for feasibility as needed.
- The **Engineering Team** provides feedback and questions, and receives lower level requirements plus any needed design run-throughs or feature training from the **Business Analyst**. The team may also raise **spikes** to aid product understanding or explore technical feasibility.
- As the work becomes ready for delivery, the **Engineering Team** splits it into **tickets** to be delivered (see [Engineering Team Process](../engineering-workflow/engineering-workflow.md)).
