# Development Career Progression framework

## Management Track (M)

| Level | Technical Depth | Scope & Impact | Leadership & Influence | Strategic Impact |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **M3** | Broad software development expertise across engineering practices, architecture, delivery, and quality. May not code daily, but understands the systems, constraints, and trade-offs well enough to steer direction. | Owns outcomes across multiple teams or a full function. Accountable for delivery quality, engineering standards, capability growth, and sustainable execution. | Aligns architects, leads, and product stakeholders around common direction. Sets expectations for how teams work and how engineering culture operates. | Defines department strategy, improves scalability and reliability, introduces better ways of working, and drives technology direction that supports long-term business goals. |
| **M2** | Strong understanding of delivery systems, operational workflows, dependencies, and organisational constraints across a department or multiple teams. | Leads multi-team or departmental execution. Owns coordination, prioritisation, governance, operational health, and the consistency of delivery across teams. | Coordinates cross-functional groups such as engineering, product, support, and operations. Creates alignment, removes friction, and ensures standards are adopted consistently. | Drives automation, process improvement, resilience, and operating maturity. Shapes structures and ways of working that improve delivery performance over time. |
| **M1** | Strong practical understanding of team delivery, quality control, resource allocation, and the technical context needed to guide engineers effectively. | Owns day-to-day team leadership. Accountable for people management, work allocation, quality outcomes, team health, and smooth execution within a team or discipline. | Coaches and supports engineers, reinforces expectations, coordinates with peers and stakeholders, and helps the team operate effectively without needing formal authority across multiple teams. | Improves team practices, raises quality and consistency, strengthens delivery discipline, and creates the conditions for better productivity, learning, and reliability. |

## Individual Contributor Track (L)

| Level | Technical Depth | Scope & Impact | Leadership Without Direct Reports | Strategic Impact |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **L5** | Defines architecture across systems or domains. Deep expertise in design, trade-offs, standards, and long-term technical direction. | Influences multiple teams, platforms, or major product areas. Accountable for technical coherence across broad areas of the organisation. | Mentors senior engineers and leads through guidance, review, and architectural direction. Sets a high bar for technical judgement and quality. | Defines long-term technical strategy, identifies major organisational improvements, and steers investment in architecture, tooling, and standards. |
| **L4** | Leads design of large systems or complex initiatives. Strong authority in architecture, standards, and delivery design without being a formal department manager. | Drives cross-team initiatives, resolves high-complexity problems, and owns major technical outcomes that span multiple workstreams. | Shapes standards, mentors others, influences decisions across teams, and provides leadership through expertise, credibility, and consistency. | Identifies and leads improvements with visible business or platform impact, including architecture changes, tooling, frameworks, and engineering practices. |
| **L3** | Designs components, modules, and significant features. Handles ambiguous technical work and supports sound implementation decisions within a team. | Leads complex projects or problem spaces within a team. Owns delivery quality and helps others navigate non-trivial work. | Mentors more junior engineers informally, supports peer decision-making, and raises the quality of delivery through review and collaboration. | Improves local architecture, code quality, maintainability, and development practices. Contributes meaningful improvements beyond immediate feature work. |
| **L2** | Owns modules or features end-to-end. Reliable across implementation, testing, debugging, and day-to-day delivery practices. | Delivers well-scoped projects or features independently within a team. Contributes consistently to team throughput and quality. | Participates actively in team collaboration, knowledge sharing, and problem solving. Builds influence through dependable execution and growing judgement. | Identifies practical process or technical improvements in their area and contributes to continuous improvement through delivery experience. |
| **L1** | Learning core systems, coding practices, tools, and delivery expectations. Works on smaller tasks with guidance and support. | Contributes to small features, fixes, support tasks, or well-defined pieces of work while building confidence and consistency. | Participates in team rituals, learns from review and feedback, and develops collaborative habits that support future growth. | Suggests small improvements, builds foundational understanding, and grows toward independent ownership over time. |

## Simple Usage in Role Specs

For progression-track role specs, add one line under Scope & Accountability:
`**Level Range:** L2-L5 (Engineer -> Senior -> Lead -> Principal)`

For fixed-level management or specialist roles, keep a single level line (e.g., `**Level:** M2` or `**Level:** L5`).
